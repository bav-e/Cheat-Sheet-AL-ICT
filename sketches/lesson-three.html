Number Systems & Conversions

Decimal ➔ Binary (Integer)

Divide by 2, read remainders up. 25 ÷ 2 = 12 R 1 ↑ 12 ÷ 2 = 6 R 0 | 6 ÷ 2 = 3 R 0 | 3 ÷ 2 = 1 R 1 | 1 ÷ 2 = 0 R 1 | Ans: 11001₂

Binary ➔ Decimal

Use place values (..., 8, 4, 2, 1). 1 1 0 0 1 ↓ ↓ ↓ ↓ ↓ (16)+(8)+(0)+(0)+(1) Ans: 25₁₀

Binary ↔ Octal

Group bits in 3s. 101110₂ ➔ 101 | 110 ↓ ↓ 5 6 Ans: 56₈

Binary ↔ Hexadecimal

Group bits in 4s. 10111110₂ ➔ 1011 | 1110 ↓ ↓ B E Ans: BE₁₆

Data Representation Concepts

Signed Number Representation

Sign-Magnitude

Left-most bit is sign (0=+, 1=-). Has two zeros.

One's Complement

Flip all bits to negate. Also has two zeros.

Two's Complement

Flip all bits + 1. Standard for PCs (efficient hardware).

Floating-Point Numbers

Concept

For scientific numbers (large/small). Uses Mantissa & Exponent.

IEEE 754 Standard

Single Precision: 8b Exp, 23b Man. Double Precision: 11b Exp, 52b Man.

Character Codes & Logic

Character Representation

BCD
- Numbers 0-9 only. Not space efficient.
ASCII
- 7-bit (128 chars). For English/PCs.
EBCDIC
- 8-bit (256 chars). For IBM Mainframes.
Unicode
- 16/32-bit. Represents all world languages.
