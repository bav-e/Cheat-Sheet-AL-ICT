II. Boolean Algebra

*Why simplify?: To reduce the number of gates, cost, and complexity of the circuit.

  • Identity: A+0=A, A.1=A
  • Annulment: A+1=1, A.0=0
  • Idempotent: A+A=A, A.A=A
  • Complement: A+A'=1, A.A'=0
  • Double Neg.: (A')' = A
  • Commutative: A+B=B+A
  • Associative: A+(B+C)=(A+B)+C
  • Distributive: A(B+C)=AB+AC
  • Absorption: A+AB=A, A(A+B)=A
  • De Morgan's:
    (AB)'=A'+B'
    (A+B)'=A'.B'

III. Karnaugh Maps (K-Maps)

K-Map Rules:

  • Cannot group zeros.
  • Groups must be a power of 2 (1, 2, 4, 8...).
  • No diagonal groups.
  • Make groups as large as possible.
  • Groups can overlap.
  • Wrap-around grouping is allowed.

Example: F(A,B,C) = Σ(3,6,7)

Group 1 (Red): AC

Group 2 (Blue): BC'

Simplified: F = AC + BC'

IV. Standard Forms (SOP & POS)

Sum of Products (SOP)

A sum of Minterms where the truth table output is '1'.

Ex: F = A'B + AB'

*Used to build a circuit for the 'ON' conditions of a problem.

Product of Sums (POS)

A product of Maxterms where the truth table output is '0'.

Ex: F = (A+B).(A'+B')

*Used to build a circuit for the 'OFF' conditions of a problem.
